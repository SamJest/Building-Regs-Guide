## Executive Read

The site is no longer at the "just get indexed" stage. Search Console shows rising visibility, a much stronger average position, and several page families with real click behaviour. The next upgrade should prioritise click-through, answer quality, and engagement before another broad programmatic expansion.

Key signals:

- Last 3 months: 4,480 clicks, 288,205 impressions, 1.55% CTR, average position 14.35.
- Momentum is positive: the first 28 days had 1,160 clicks from 78,494 impressions at average position 23.97; the latest 26 days had 1,960 clicks from 122,459 impressions at average position 8.99.
- UK traffic is the useful traffic: 4,397 clicks and 217,039 impressions from the United Kingdom.
- Desktop still underperforms: 1,863 clicks from 193,633 impressions, 0.96% CTR, versus mobile at 2.67% CTR.
- The biggest immediate leak is high-rank, zero-click HMO/Article 4 demand.
- The site already has about 34k generated pages in the sitemap, so expansion should be gated by quality and CTR fixes.

## Implemented Upgrade Batch

Recommended implementation scope for this cycle:

- Add new data-led HMO/Article 4 local-search pages for Milton Keynes, West Northamptonshire/Northampton, and Nuneaton/Warwickshire.
- Upgrade Tamworth, Stafford, Charnwood and Leicestershire HMO local-search metadata around Article 4, HMO planning and permitted-development risk.
- Move official-source checks earlier on local-search and GSC recovery pages.
- Make GSC recovery pages answer the recovered query directly before routing users onward.
- Add visible save/print/share result-capture blocks to high-intent guide pages, not only tool results.
- Add specific metadata overrides for low-CTR conservation-area and council pages.

## Expansion Gate

Do not expand broadly until the following pass:

- Metadata quality audit passes.
- Local-search quality audit passes.
- Duplicate/content validation passes or sampled warnings are reviewed.
- Current high-impression low-CTR pages have rebuilt with updated titles, descriptions and first-screen answers.
- Sitemap URL count and generated HTML count are reconciled after rebuild.

If those gates pass, the next expansion should be a controlled 2,000-3,000 page batch focused on proven modifiers: HMO/Article 4, conservation areas, outbuildings, porches, fences/walls, dropped kerbs and local planning-permission routes.

## Monitoring

Monitor weekly in GSC:

- UK clicks and impressions.
- Desktop CTR.
- HMO/Article 4 CTR, especially Tamworth, Stafford, Milton Keynes, West Northamptonshire, Charnwood and Leicestershire.
- Conservation-area CTR for Glasgow City, Westminster, Portsmouth and Leeds.
- Local-search CTR as a page type.
- Pages with impressions but 0-1 clicks.
- New local-search page indexing and sitemap inclusion.
