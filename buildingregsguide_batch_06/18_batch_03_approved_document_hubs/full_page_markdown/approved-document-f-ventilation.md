# Approved Document F: Ventilation

Approved Document F is the practical guidance route for **adequate ventilation to buildings, rooms and wet areas, including extract and background ventilation**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- bathrooms
- kitchens
- utility rooms
- extensions
- loft conversions
- window replacement
- insulation upgrades that reduce air leakage

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- extract fan specification
- ventilation calculations/strategy
- commissioning certificates where needed
- trickle vent/window details
- handover/user information

## Common red flags

- new or moved bathroom/kitchen
- sealed-up fireplaces or draught reduction
- highly insulated retrofit
- condensation or mould history
- rooms without openable windows

## Related Approved Documents

This Part often overlaps with Part C, Part G, Part L, Part O. The page must not present any Part in isolation where a normal project usually crosses several requirements.
