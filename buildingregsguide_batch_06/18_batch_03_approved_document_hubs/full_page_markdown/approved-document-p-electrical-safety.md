# Approved Document P: Electrical safety

Approved Document P is the practical guidance route for **electrical safety in dwellings and associated building work**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- new circuits
- consumer unit changes
- bathroom/kitchen electrics
- outdoor power
- garden rooms
- solar/battery/EV work

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- Electrical Installation Certificate
- Building Regulations compliance certificate or notice
- minor works certificate where relevant
- installer details
- circuit schedule

## Common red flags

- DIY notifiable electrical work
- work in special locations
- new consumer unit
- outdoor circuits
- missing certificate when selling/remortgaging

## Related Approved Documents

This Part often overlaps with Part J, Part L, Part S. The page must not present any Part in isolation where a normal project usually crosses several requirements.
