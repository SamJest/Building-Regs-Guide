# Approved Document L: Conservation of fuel and power

Approved Document L is the practical guidance route for **energy performance, insulation, heating efficiency, airtightness, glazing and services efficiency**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- extensions
- loft conversions
- garage conversions
- windows and doors
- boilers and heating
- insulation retrofits
- solar/low-carbon upgrades

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- U-value calculations
- SAP/energy calculations if required
- insulation specification
- heating controls details
- air-tightness/commissioning information
- photos before covering insulation

## Common red flags

- large glazed extensions
- solid wall insulation
- thermal bridges
- uninsulated converted garages
- changing heating systems
- using old Part L assumptions after 2026 updates

## Related Approved Documents

This Part often overlaps with Part F, Part O, Part S. The page must not present any Part in isolation where a normal project usually crosses several requirements.
