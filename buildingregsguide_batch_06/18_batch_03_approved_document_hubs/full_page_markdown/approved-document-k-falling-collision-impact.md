# Approved Document K: Protection from falling, collision and impact

Approved Document K is the practical guidance route for **stairs, ramps, guards, glazing collision risk and protection from falling**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- loft stairs
- new staircases
- balconies
- rooflights
- large glazed doors
- raised decking
- split-level alterations

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- stair dimensions
- guarding/balustrade spec
- safety glazing location plan
- manufacturer data
- photos before completion

## Common red flags

- steep loft stairs
- low guarding
- unprotected drops
- large panes near doors/floors
- roof access or balcony works

## Related Approved Documents

This Part often overlaps with Part B, Part M, Part Q. The page must not present any Part in isolation where a normal project usually crosses several requirements.
