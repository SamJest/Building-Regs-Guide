# Approved Document H: Drainage and waste disposal

Approved Document H is the practical guidance route for **foul water, rainwater drainage, solid waste storage and related drainage arrangements**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- extensions near drains
- new bathrooms/kitchens
- driveways and surface water
- garden rooms with drainage
- basements
- new rainwater connections

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- existing/proposed drainage layout
- inspection chamber locations
- sewer build-over approval where relevant
- photos of drains before backfill
- air/admittance/venting details

## Common red flags

- building over or near a public sewer
- moving inspection chambers
- new foul drainage to outbuildings
- surface water discharged to combined sewer unnecessarily
- unknown drain ownership
