# Approved Document O: Overheating

Approved Document O is the practical guidance route for **limiting unwanted solar gains and providing means to remove excess heat in new residential buildings**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- new dwellings
- some residential change-of-use work
- large glazing design checks
- top-floor/roof-space homes

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- overheating assessment
- glazing areas and orientation
- ventilation/opening strategy
- shading details

## Common red flags

- large south/west-facing glazing
- restricted window opening due to noise/security
- single-aspect flats
- roof rooms with high solar gain

## Related Approved Documents
