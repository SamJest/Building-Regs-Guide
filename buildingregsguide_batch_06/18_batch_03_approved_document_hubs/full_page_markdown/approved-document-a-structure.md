# Approved Document A: Structure

Approved Document A is the practical guidance route for **structural safety: loads, ground movement, disproportionate collapse and stability of buildings and building work**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- load-bearing wall removal
- chimney breast removal
- loft conversions
- extensions
- basements
- underpinning
- new structural openings

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- structural engineer calculations
- beam/steel schedules
- foundation notes
- existing/proposed plans
- site photos before cover-up
- inspection sign-off for structural stages

## Common red flags

- any load-bearing element being removed
- unknown wall construction
- large openings into old walls
- work near party walls or shared structures
- cracks, movement, subsidence history

## Related Approved Documents

This Part often overlaps with Part B, Part C, Part K, Part L. The page must not present any Part in isolation where a normal project usually crosses several requirements.
