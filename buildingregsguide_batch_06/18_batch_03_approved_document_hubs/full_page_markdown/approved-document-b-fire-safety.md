# Approved Document B: Fire safety

Approved Document B is the practical guidance route for **means of warning and escape, internal fire spread, external fire spread and fire service access**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- loft conversions
- garage conversions
- open-plan layouts
- extensions affecting escape routes
- flats and higher-risk buildings
- replacement doors in flats

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- fire strategy notes
- smoke/heat alarm specification
- protected stair/escape route notes
- fire door schedule where relevant
- completion certificates for alarms/electrics

## Common red flags

- new room in the roof
- open-plan ground floor below upper-storey bedrooms
- work to flats/common parts
- removing doors that protected escape routes
- combustible cladding or balcony alterations

## Related Approved Documents

This Part often overlaps with Part A, Part F, Part K, Part M. The page must not present any Part in isolation where a normal project usually crosses several requirements.
