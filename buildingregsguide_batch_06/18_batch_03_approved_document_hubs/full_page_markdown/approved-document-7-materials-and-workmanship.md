# Approved Document 7: Materials and workmanship

Approved Document 7 is the practical guidance route for **suitability of materials and workmanship standards used to meet building regulation requirements**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- all regulated work
- product substitutions
- insulation/fire products
- structural materials
- windows/doors

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- product datasheets
- CE/UKCA or equivalent evidence where applicable
- installer records
- substitution log
- photos of installed products before cover-up

## Common red flags

- unapproved substitutions
- missing product data
- using unsuitable reclaimed materials
- installer cannot evidence specification

## Related Approved Documents

This Part often overlaps with Part A, Part B, Part C, Part L. The page must not present any Part in isolation where a normal project usually crosses several requirements.
