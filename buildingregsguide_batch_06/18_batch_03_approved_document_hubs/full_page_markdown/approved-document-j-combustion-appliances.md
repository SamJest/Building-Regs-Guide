# Approved Document J: Combustion appliances and fuel storage

Approved Document J is the practical guidance route for **safe combustion appliances, flues, hearths, ventilation, carbon monoxide safety and fuel storage**. This page should help a homeowner understand when this Part is likely to matter, what evidence to keep, and which project pages to read next.

This is not a substitute for a designer, structural engineer, registered installer, building control body or the official Approved Document. Codex should render this as a plain-English hub with prominent links to the official source and to project-specific checks.

## When this Part commonly appears

- boilers
- wood burners
- chimneys/flues
- oil/gas/LPG appliances
- fireplaces
- biomass appliances

## What the homeowner normally needs to prove

- Gas Safe/OFTEC/HETAS certificate where applicable
- flue specification
- CO alarm details
- ventilation provision
- commissioning certificate

## Common red flags

- new stove or flue
- boiler relocation
- sealed rooms with combustion appliances
- blocked chimneys
- DIY fuel appliance work
