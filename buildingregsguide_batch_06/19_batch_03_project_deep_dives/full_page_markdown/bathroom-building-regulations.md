# Bathroom building regulations

This page explains the building-control route, likely Approved Documents, inspection points and evidence to keep for this project. It should be written for homeowners before they instruct a builder, not for professionals doing design calculations.

## Likely building-control route

Minor like-for-like changes may not need an application, but new drainage, electrical work, ventilation and structural changes can trigger approval/certification.

## Approved Documents likely to matter

Likely relevant documents: **Part F, Part G, Part H, Part P, Part K**.

Codex should render these as linked chips pointing to the Approved Document hubs. The wording must say "likely" unless a rule is certain from official guidance.

## Inspection stages to plan around

- drainage alterations
- ventilation installation
- electrics certification
- final completion

## Evidence folder checklist

- Written scope of work.
- Existing and proposed plans where applicable.
- Product specifications for materials that affect fire, structure, insulation, ventilation, drainage, glazing or services.
- Installer certificates for competent-person work.
- Photos before work is covered up.
- Building control inspection records and final/completion certificate.

## Common failure points

- new bathroom without extract
- DIY electrics in special location
- poor falls on waste pipes
- unvented hot water cylinder work
