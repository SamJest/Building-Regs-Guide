# Loft Conversions

## Plain-English answer

Project page focused on structure, stairs, protected escape, fire doors, alarms, ventilation and insulation. The page should answer the user's immediate question first, then move them into the correct next action: building control application, competent person route, evidence recovery, or further professional checking.

For most domestic users, the key point is that building regulations approval is about whether the work complies technically. It is not the same as planning permission, and one approval does not automatically replace the other. The page must repeat this clearly near the top because many users arrive from planning-search intent and assume both systems are connected.

## What the user needs to decide

Use this order:

1. Is the building a higher-risk building or part of one?
2. Is the work exempt, competent-person self-certified, or clearly notifiable?
3. Has work already started or finished?
4. Does the project affect structure, fire safety, drainage, insulation, ventilation, controlled services or electrical safety?
5. Is separate planning, listed building consent, leaseholder/freeholder consent, party wall procedure, sewer approval or other permission likely?

## Likely route

If the work affects controlled building work, the safe default is to check with a building control body before starting. For simple domestic work, a building notice may be possible, but for complex, structural, unusual, higher-risk, or evidence-sensitive work, full plans are usually the better user journey because the drawings and specifications are checked before work gets too far.

Where a competent person scheme applies, the user may not need to apply separately, but the page must tell them to keep the compliance certificate. This is especially important for selling a home, mortgage checks, insurance queries, and future alterations.

## Evidence to prepare

- structural calculations for floor/roof changes

- stairs layout
- insulation and ventilation details

## Inspection and sign-off prompts

- commencement

- insulation

The implementation should render these as a checklist component with a warning: do not cover foundations, drains, beams, insulation, fire protection or services until the relevant inspection/evidence route is agreed.

## Approved Documents likely to matter

Likely Approved Document routing: A, B, F, K, L, M, P.

This must be presented as a guide, not a definitive legal answer. The Approved Documents explain common ways to meet the regulations, but a compliant design can use other evidence where suitable.

## FAQ block

### Does this replace planning permission?
No. Building regulations approval and planning permission are different systems. A project can need one, both, or neither depending on the work and property.

### Can I start first and sort approval later?
Do not present that as safe. Some routes must happen before work starts, and hidden work can make inspection, certification and sale evidence harder.

### Can my builder sort it?
A builder, designer or installer may help, but the site should explain that the homeowner/client still needs to make sure the correct route and evidence exist.

### What is the safest next step?
Use the site checker, collect the evidence listed above, and contact the relevant building control body or a registered competent professional before starting rule-sensitive work.
