Approved Document A: Structure

Homeowner-focused explanation of Approved Document A: Structure and which project pages should link to it.

Short answer

Homeowner-focused explanation of Approved Document A: Structure and which project pages should link to it.

This guide helps you prepare the right questions. It does not grant approval, replace building control or remove the need for competent design and installation.

Approved Documents are guidance for ways to comply in England, not the only possible method. Part F and Part L pages are version-aware because 2026 editions may affect future projects while earlier versions can still matter for older work.

What usually triggers extra checks

  • Structural changes, new openings, beams, altered load paths or work that changes fire escape routes.
  • New or altered drainage, ventilation, insulation, glazing, heating, electrics or controlled services.
  • Work already completed without clear records, inspections or certificates.
  • Anything outside ordinary domestic England guidance, including Wales, Scotland, Northern Ireland or higher-risk building routes.

Route options to discuss

Most homeowners are comparing full plans, a building notice, competent person self-certification, regularisation for historic work, or an early specialist route. The best fit depends on risk, timing, drawings, installer registration and what building control wants to inspect.

Evidence to keep

  • Application references, notices, plans, specifications and structural calculations.
  • Inspection dates, site photos before work is covered, installer details and product records.
  • Completion certificate, competent person certificate, warranties and handover notes.
  • A dated note of which official source/version you checked before work started.

Mistakes to avoid

Do not assume planning permission, permitted development or a builder's quote answers the building regulations question. Do not cover up work before required inspections. Do not rely on a certificate claim without checking who issues it and how you will receive a copy.

Use this page to prepare

  • Pick a likely building-control route.
  • List drawings, calculations and certificates.
  • Plan inspections before work is covered.
  • Save evidence for sale or remortgage.
